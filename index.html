Litreature Review

Online voting and grading systems are digital tools that enable voters to cast their votes and teachers to rate student performance remotely. Despite challenges like security risks, technical issues, and accessibility, the use of these systems is expected to grow in Sri Lanka. The proposal aims to revolutionize democratic processes by introducing a comprehensive digital framework that allows citizens to exercise their right to vote and facilitates transparent and informed evaluation of candidates. By embracing digital improvements, Sri Lanka can usher in a new era of efficiency, inclusion, and credibility in its electoral sector.

Online voting systems are gaining attention due to their potential to enhance efficiency, accessibility, and security in the political process. Facial recognition technology is a recent breakthrough in this area. This literature review provides a comprehensive background on online voting systems, highlighting the challenges faced by traditional voting methods, which are often conducted in person at polling places using paper-based technologies. The use of facial recognition technology in online voting systems is expected to further enhance election efficiency and integrity.

Time-Consuming - The traditional voting process can take a lot of time, both for voters and election authorities. Some individuals can be discouraged from voting if there are long lineups at the polls.

Prone to Mistakes - Paper ballots are prone to human mistakes, such as incorrect marking or incorrect counting. This can erode voter trust in the election by sparking arguments and recount initiatives.

Accessibility Issues - Not all eligible voters may have access to conventional voting procedures. It may be difficult for older voters, those with impairments, or residents in rural locations to cast their ballots in person.

Cardinot, dos Santos, and Ferreira (2019) propose a blockchain-based data gathering mechanism for online voting systems, ensuring the integrity and trustworthiness of collected voting data. Dimitrakakis and Fotakis (2020) focus on developing efficient data processing and vote counting mechanisms for online voting systems, proposing algorithms and techniques to handle large volumes of data accurately and quickly. Sui, Feng, and Hu (2021) present a privacy-preserving data aggregation technique for online voting systems, protecting individual votes while aggregating data for accurate counting and analysis. Overall, these studies provide valuable insights into the potential of blockchain technology in online voting systems.

By strengthening the democratic, inclusive and transparent qualities of elections, online voting technology has the potential to completely revolutionize the way they are conducted. By analyzing and displaying the massive amount of data that an online voting system can generate, insights into voter behavior, preferences and patterns can be gained. Data analysis can reveal voter turnout trends, effectiveness of outreach initiatives, and voter turnout.

Research Gap

To ensure the security, accuracy, and accessibility of online voting systems for elections, it is crucial to identify and close research gaps. Blockchain technology has been used to improve transparency, prevent fraud, improve data security, and verify results. However, there are significant security risks associated with electronic voting, such as potential manipulation of votes. Despite its potential advantages, nationwide adoption of electronic voting is still a distant goal. Blockchain technology can effectively address these risks, making the main difference between the two systems clear.

Traditional voting systems have a central authority, making it easy for changes. However, blockchain-based electronic voting systems, like Bitcoin, maintain data across multiple nodes, making it impossible to alter or eliminate votes. This distributed nature reduces risks associated with electronic voting and makes voting tamper-proof. The infrastructure for blockchain-based voting must be completely distributed, and no single entity, including the government, can have complete control over the system.

Online voting systems face significant challenges in security and authentication, with the development of reliable tools for safe voter authentication being crucial. End-to-end verifiability is still a challenge, and thorough threat assessments are essential for identifying potential flaws. Standardized testing frameworks for security assessments are also crucial. Protecting voters' identities and privacy is crucial, and cutting-edge cryptographic methods should be developed to address these issues. Strategies to safeguard voter privacy after the election are also essential. Accessibility for all voters, including those with impairments and linguistic and cultural barriers, is also crucial. Effective auditing procedures are necessary for internet voting reliability and fraud prevention.

Research Problem

The implementation of an online voting and grading system in Sri Lanka is a potential solution due to the growing use of technology in modern society. However, there are complex problems to be addressed, including technology infrastructure, security, accessibility, and voter involvement. This study aims to address these issues and provide in-depth analysis and creative solutions. The traditional paper-based voting system in Sri Lanka has drawbacks, such as the time-consuming and expensive process of gathering and registering candidate and voter data. Additionally, the database entry process takes time and money, leading to errors and delays. Addressing these issues is crucial for Sri Lanka's democratic development and the larger discussion around technology in election systems in many international contexts.

The paper-based voting system in Sri Lanka is a time-consuming and expensive process that involves printing data forms, staffing registration booths, and promoting registration days. This process also leads to errors and delays, as well as environmental damage and global warming due to paper consumption. Human errors in data entry are common, making it challenging to ensure 100% accuracy. To address these challenges, Sri Lanka should adopt an online election voting system that streamlines the entire process, making it more efficient and cost-effective. The main issue is ensuring accurate voter authentication using NIC and face IDs, and if user credentials mismatch, the system should restrict access by login. Protection of the system is crucial to achieve high productivity and security.

The development of an online voting and ranking system for elections holds great promise for increasing productivity and reducing time consumption. However, there is no single application that provides all necessary features for registration, voting, and evaluation while maintaining high security. Sri Lanka's electoral system lacks a comprehensive and secure online platform. The vote counting procedure should be transparent, accurate, and public, with results announced immediately. To avoid fraud or tampering, the voting system should be secure, including ballot box security, voter identification, authentication, and cyber threats. Data compression techniques can help minimize data size and reduce visualization time.

What is QUICK POLL..?

Online voting and grading systems are digital tools that allow voters to cast their votes and teachers to rate student performance remotely over the Internet. Online voting and rating systems also come with challenges such as security risks, technical issues, and accessibility and enrollment issues. Despite these challenges, the use of online voting and rating systems in Sri Lanka is expected to continue to grow as technology continues to improve and more people embrace digital tools for everyday tasks.

In an era marked by rapid technological advancements, traditional methods of conducting elections and evaluating candidates have faced challenges that call for innovative solutions. By embracing these digital improvements, Sri Lanka has the potential to usher in a new era of efficiency, inclusion and credibility in its electoral sector. This article examines the complexities of implementing such a system, exploring its benefits, challenges and imperatives for evolving democratic ideals in Sri Lanka in the 21st century.

Better Authentication
Enhancing voter authentication is one of the main uses of facial recognition in online elections. The technology can lower the possibility of fraudulent voting and make sure that every voter is eligible by using face biometrics to confirm their identification.

Convenience and Accessibility
For voters, especially those with impairments, online voting can be more convenient and accessible thanks to face recognition technology. It makes voting easier and does away with the need for intricate identification processes.

Enhanced Security
Online voting systems are made more secure by integrating facial recognition. It can identify and stop identity theft or impersonation, making it harder for bad actors to influence the outcome of elections.

Privacy Issues
Face recognition has many advantages, but it also presents privacy issues. To safeguard people's right to privacy, the collecting and storage of biometric data must be done with extreme caution.

Online voting methods make use of the internet and digital technologies to let voters participate from a distance. These methods have several benefits, including improved accessibility for voters who live far or have disabilities, lower costs, and the potential for real-time results. They do, however, also bring up issues with privacy and security. Using face recognition technology, people may be recognized or verified based on their facial traits. Applications have been found for it in many other industries, including security, access control, and even online elections. Recent improvements in facial recognition algorithms, especially those based on deep learning, have greatly increased accuracy and speed. Face recognition may now be incorporated into many different applications thanks to these improvements.
